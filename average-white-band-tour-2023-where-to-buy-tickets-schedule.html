It’s time to “Pick Up The Pieces” one last time.

Starting on June 9, the legendary Average White Band will launch their international farewell ‘Let’s Go Round Again One Last Time’ Tour in North America.

The stay in the U.S. will be short for the British outfit though.

At the time of publication, the funk, soul and R&B group featuring original members Alan Gorrie and Onnie McIntyre only have five stateside shows lined up.

This run of concerts includes two gigs at New York City’s City Winery on Friday, Aug. 18 at 6 p.m. and 9 p.m.

Will there be more opportunities for fans to see see them live on this last go-round?

It’s hard to tell — they just announced their confirmed last performances in Scotland and the U.K.

There’s no word on the U.S. and Canada yet but this might really be it.

And while it’s certainly emotional for the band to say goodbye to the stage, they are excited to play for fans one last time.

“That it’s finally coming to an end is going to mean a highly emotional tour next year, but one which will mean we bow out at the top level,” Gorrie said.

“Please join us in a final celebration of that journey, and to a last hurrah of epic proportions with your presence and your aye-ready appreciation!”

Want to hear “Work To Do,” “Cut The Cake” and “School Boy Crush” live one last time?
