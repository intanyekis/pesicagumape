Rory MacDonald is a Canadian mixed martial artist that appeared in the Welterweight division of Ultimate Fighting Championship. Rory MacDonald trained Brazilian jiu-jitsu under the leadership of David Lea at Toshido Fighting Arts Academy and moved to Tristar Gym. He started his professional debut at age 16, when he won against Thierry Thiara during an Extreme Fighting Challenge bout. He moved to the King of the Cage as MacDonald defeated Ken Tran. Then, he switched from lightweight to was division when he won against Elmer Waterhen. As he compiled a 9-0 professional record, he signed up for UFC. MacDonald's first fight in UFC when he was slated to face Mike Guymon during the UFC Fight Night 20 and won by submission in the first round. Other opponents that he faced are Nate Diaz, Carlos Condit, Claude Patrick, and Che Mills. Rory Joseph MacDonald was born on July 22, 1989 in Quesnel, British Columbia, Canada.
