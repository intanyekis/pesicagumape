Who is Jerrell Powe?

The defensive tackle made 89 tackles during his senior season of competition, nine of which were quarterbacks. He also forced five fumbles. Powe eventually received All-American honors from Parade and USA Today as a result.

Powe was ranked as the nation’s third-best defensive line prospect when he entered college. Although he was courted by many schools, he chose to enrol at Ole Miss instead of Auburn, LSU, or Texas A&M.

Powe played in 37 games from 2008 to 2010 and recorded 69 tackles, seven sacks, and an interception. He was recognised as Second-Team All-Southeastern Conference in 2009 and 2010.

Jerrell Powe draft

Powe was formerly considered to be the best nose tackle prospect in college football, but his performance suffered, and by the 2011 NFL Draft, he was only considered to be a second- or third-round player.

Powe was 24 at the time as well, which is a high age for an NFL rookie. In the end, he was chosen in the sixth round by the Kansas City Chiefs.
